# Quality & Non-Conformance Management

Senior quality engineering expertise for regulated manufacturing (FDA 21 CFR 820, IATF 16949, AS9100, ISO 13485).

## When to Use

- Investigating an NCR from incoming inspection, in-process, final test, or field
- Performing root cause analysis using 5-Why, Ishikawa, fault tree, or 8D
- Determining disposition (use-as-is, rework, repair, scrap, RTV)
- Creating or reviewing a CAPA plan
- Interpreting SPC control-chart signals
- Preparing for or responding to a regulatory audit finding

## How It Works

1. Detect through inspection, SPC alert, or customer complaint
2. Contain affected material immediately (quarantine, hold, shipment stop)
3. Classify severity by safety impact and regulatory requirements
4. Investigate root cause with structured methodology
5. Determine disposition via MRB or approved authority
6. Implement corrective action, verify effectiveness, close CAPA with evidence

## Decision Frameworks

- **Disposition:** safety/regulatory → customer-specific requirements → functional impact → reworkability → supplier accountability
- **RCA selection:** 5 Whys for simple; Ishikawa + 5 Whys for multi-category; 8D for recurring process issues; FTA for safety-critical events
- **CAPA effectiveness:** verify implementation; monitor 90 days/3 lots/one audit cycle; confirm zero recurrence; review leading indicators
- **Inspection levels:** tightened for new suppliers/rejections; reduced/skip-lot after sustained acceptance
- **Supplier escalation:** SCAR → watch → controlled shipping → new source qualification → ASL removal

## Key Guardrails

- Human error is never a root cause; "retrain" is the weakest corrective action
- "In spec" is not the same as "in control"
- Do not adjust a stable process for common-cause variation
