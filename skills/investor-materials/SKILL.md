# Investor Materials

Build investor-facing materials that are consistent, credible, and easy to defend.

## When to Use

- Creating or revising a pitch deck
- Writing an investor memo or one-pager
- Building a financial model, milestone plan, or use-of-funds table
- Answering accelerator or incubator application questions
- Aligning multiple fundraising docs around one source of truth

## Golden Rule

All investor materials must agree. Confirm one source of truth before writing: traction metrics; pricing and revenue assumptions; raise size and instrument; use of funds; team bios and titles; milestones and timelines. If numbers conflict, stop and resolve before drafting.

## Core Workflow

1. Inventory canonical facts
2. Identify missing assumptions
3. Choose the asset type
4. Draft with explicit logic
5. Cross-check every number against the source of truth

## Asset Guidance

**Pitch Deck:** company + wedge; problem; solution; product/demo; market; business model; traction; team; competition/differentiation; ask; use of funds/milestones; appendix. Pair with `frontend-slides` for web-native decks.

**One-Pager / Memo:** one clean sentence for what the company does; why now; traction early; precise ask; verifiable claims.

**Financial Model:** explicit assumptions; bear/base/bull cases; clean revenue logic; milestone-linked spending; sensitivity analysis where decisions hinge on assumptions.

**Accelerator Applications:** answer exactly what is asked; prioritize traction, insight, and team advantage; avoid puffery; keep metrics consistent with deck and model.

## Red Flags

- Unverifiable claims
- Fuzzy market sizing without assumptions
- Inconsistent team roles or titles
- Revenue math that does not sum cleanly
- Inflated certainty where assumptions are fragile

## Quality Gate

Before delivering: every number matches the source of truth; use of funds and revenue layers sum correctly; assumptions are visible; the story is clear without hype; the asset is defensible in a partner meeting.
