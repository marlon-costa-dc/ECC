# content-engine

## When to use
- Writing X posts or threads
- Drafting LinkedIn posts or launch updates
- Scripting short-form video or YouTube explainers
- Repurposing articles, podcasts, demos, docs, or notes into public content
- Building a launch sequence or ongoing content system

## What to do
1. Identify the source material before drafting.
2. Reuse or build a voice profile with `brand-voice` when consistency matters.
3. Extract 3–7 atomic claims or scenes from the source.
4. Rank them by sharpness, novelty, and proof.
5. Adapt one strong idea per output to each platform.
6. Strip platform-shaped filler and generic hype.
7. Run the quality gate before delivering.

## Critical rules
- Start from source material, not generic post formulas.
- Adapt the format for the platform, not the persona.
- One post carries one actual claim.
- Specificity beats adjectives.
- No engagement bait unless the user explicitly asks for it.
- Delete and rewrite generic hype: "game-changer", "revolutionary", "cutting-edge", "in today's rapidly evolving landscape".
- Avoid forced casualness on LinkedIn, fake lessons, praise-stacking, and journey filler.
- Every draft must sound like the intended author, not the platform stereotype.
- Any CTA must be earned and user-approved.

## Example (if needed)

Repurposing a product demo:

- Anchor asset: 3-minute demo showing the new sync flow.
- Claims: (1) sync now resolves conflicts automatically, (2) setup drops from 12 steps to 2, (3) users keep full audit history.
- X: one post on the 12→2 step drop with a screenshot.
- LinkedIn: short story about why the old setup burned support hours.
- YouTube: 90-second explainer opening with the conflict-resolution example.
