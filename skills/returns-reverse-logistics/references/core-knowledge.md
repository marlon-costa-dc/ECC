# Returns & Reverse Logistics — Core Knowledge

## Returns Policy Logic

- **Standard windows:** 30 days general merchandise; 15 days electronics; non-returnable perishables; 30-90 days furniture/mattresses; extended holiday windows
- **Condition:** original packaging, all accessories, no signs of use beyond reasonable inspection
- **Receipt:** POS lookup by card/loyalty/phone; gift receipts = exchange/store credit at purchase price; no-receipt returns capped ($50-75, 3 per 12 months) at lowest recent selling price
- **Restocking fees:** opened electronics ~15%; special-order 20-25%; waived for defective/fulfilment errors
- **Cross-channel:** BORIS refunds at original purchase price; flag for return-to-DC or store inventory
- **International:** duty drawback requires re-export proof; offer returnless refund when shipping > 40% of value
- **Exceptions:** price-match, buyer's remorse, defective outside warranty, loyalty-tier overrides

## Inspection and Grading

- **Grade A (Like New):** packaging intact, all accessories, no use signs, functional. Restock/new or open box. 85-100% recovery. 45-90 sec inspection.
- **Grade B (Good):** minor cosmetic wear, packaging may be damaged, functional. Open box/renewed. 60-80% recovery.
- **Grade C (Fair):** visible wear, missing minor accessories, functional. Secondary channels. 30-50% recovery.
- **Grade D (Salvage/Parts):** non-functional/heavily damaged. Parts or materials recovery 5-15%, else recycle/destroy.

Electronics require functional testing (2-4 min); apparel focuses on stains/odour/tags; cosmetics/personal care rarely restockable once opened.

## Disposition Decision Trees

- **Restock as new:** only Grade A complete packaging; relabeling may trigger FTC issues
- **Open box:** Grade A damaged packaging or Grade B; repackaging cost $5-15
- **Refurbish:** viable when cost < 40% of refurbished selling price and channel exists
- **Liquidate:** Grade C and some Grade B; pallet auctions/wholesalers; 5-20% recovery. Do not mix categories.
- **Donate:** when FMV > liquidation recovery and tax liability can use deduction; protect brand
- **Destroy:** recalled, counterfeit, regulatory disposal (batteries/hazmat/WEEE), brand-sensitive goods. Certificate of destruction required.

## Fraud Detection

- **Wardrobing:** wear and return; indicators: event-clustered returns, deodorant/makeup traces, stretched fabric
- **Receipt fraud:** found/stolen/fabricated receipts; counter with ID requirements and payment-method matching
- **Swap fraud:** returning counterfeit/cheaper/broken item in original packaging; counter with serial-number verification and weight checks
- **Serial returners:** > 30% return rate or > $5,000 annual returns; segment by reason, condition, net LTV
- **Bracketing:** intentional multi-size/color ordering; address with fit tech, free exchanges, education
- **Price arbitrage:** returning discount purchase for full-price credit; tie refund to actual purchase price
- **Organized retail crime (ORC):** coordinated theft-and-return; report to loss prevention

## Vendor Recovery

- **RTV:** accumulate defective units to vendor thresholds ($200-500), obtain authorization, ship, track credit. Do not miss claim windows (often 90 days).
- **Defect claims:** when defect rate exceeds agreement threshold (2-5%); aggregate photos, inspection notes, complaint data by SKU
- **Chargebacks:** vendor-caused issues (wrong item, mislabel, packaging failures) with documented compliance program
- **Credit vs replacement vs write-off:** pursue credit with responsive vendors; negotiate replacement for difficult collections; write off small claims with critical suppliers

## Warranty Management

- **Warranty vs return:** return = purchase reversal (short window, any reason); warranty = defect report within coverage period (90 days to lifetime)
- **Retailer vs manufacturer:** retailer handles return window; manufacturer handles warranty; extended warranties handled by third-party provider
